About the Author

The story behind the message and the heart behind this book.

Author

Bradley Hobbs

Bradley Hobbs is a Christian author whose greatest desire is to point people toward Jesus Christ through biblical truth, thoughtful teaching, and practical encouragement. His writing is rooted in a deep conviction that every believer is called to walk in humility, pursue holiness, and submit every area of life to the authority of God's Word.

Having witnessed both the beauty of faithful leadership and the pain that can result when spiritual authority is misunderstood or misused, Bradley is passionate about encouraging believers to distinguish between human influence and the authority that belongs to Christ alone. His writing challenges readers to examine their hearts honestly while continually returning to the grace, mercy, and lordship of Jesus.

Who Gave You Authority? was written not to criticize the Church, but to strengthen it. The book invites readers to explore what Scripture teaches about leadership, humility, discernment, spiritual gifts, character, and servant hearted ministry. Rather than placing confidence in personalities or positions, Bradley encourages believers to place their complete confidence in Jesus Christ, the only true Head of His Church.

Beyond writing, Bradley has devoted much of his life to serving others through education, ministry, mentoring, and community involvement. He believes that leadership begins with serving, influence grows through integrity, and every opportunity entrusted by God should ultimately reflect His glory.

His prayer is simple. That every book he writes would faithfully proclaim the truth of Scripture, encourage believers to grow in their relationship with Christ, and remind readers that genuine authority is always expressed through humility, love, and obedience to God.

"The greatest authority any believer will ever possess is the privilege of faithfully serving the One who possesses all authority."

Mission

Why I Write

Every page I write is intended to glorify Jesus Christ and encourage believers to grow deeper in their relationship with Him. My hope is never to build a platform for myself, but to point people toward the Savior who alone is worthy of our worship, our trust, and our obedience. If my words help someone return to Scripture, seek Christ more earnestly, or serve others more faithfully, then they have accomplished their purpose.
